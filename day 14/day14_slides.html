Vocabulary üìö

Modem vs Router

Modem: Connects your house to the internet (talks to your Internet Service Provider like Comcast or AT&T).

Router: Takes that internet connection and shares it with all your devices (creates your home WiFi network).

Simple Analogy: The modem is like a water pipe bringing water to your house. The router is like the sprinkler system that spreads that water to different parts of your yard.

Static vs Dynamic Websites üìÑ

Static Website

Definition: A website where every visitor sees the exact same content. The pages are pre-built and don't change unless you edit the code.

Think of it like: A printed poster on a wall - everyone who looks at it sees the same thing.

üìÑ Static Sites

  • Same for everyone
  • Fast to load
  • Simple to make
  • HTML, CSS, JavaScript only
Examples:
Portfolio sites, landing pages, restaurant menus

üîÑ Dynamic Sites

  • Different for each user
  • Has a database
  • More complex
  • Needs server-side code
Examples:
Facebook, Netflix, Instagram
Slide 11 of 17

Why We're Building Static Sites üéØ

Perfect for Beginners!

  • Simple to understand - Just HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
  • Fast to build - See results immediately
  • Free to host - GitHub Pages hosts them for free!
  • No database needed - Less complexity to worry about
  • Great for portfolios - Show off your projects
Real Talk: Many professional developers prefer static sites when possible because they're faster, more secure, and cheaper to run. Sites like documentation pages, portfolios, and landing pages are often static.
